01 — Why Whoosh

A market leader betting on its own hardware

≈45% of the Russian micromobility market
module · battery · scooter Vertically integrated — it builds the whole stack
  • A real company with public data. Strategy and operations are documented, so the analysis is grounded in facts, not guesses.
  • The right size to study. Not a tiny family business, not an opaque giant — knowable but not over-known.
  • Builds its own IoT module, battery and scooter frame. That vertical integration is the whole story.
  • Exactly the assignment's sweet spot. Enough public signal to reason rigorously about a live strategic bet.

02 — Mission, vision & the insight

The moat is proprietary hardware — but hardware alone doesn't pay

Mission

Micromobility built on safety, adaptability, and the value of time — giving cities back time for a fuller life.

Vision

The 15-minute city: any resident, anywhere, within 15 minutes — micromobility as core urban infrastructure by 2030.

The insight that drives our project

Whoosh's edge is its proprietary 7th-gen "thinking" module. But a piece of hardware is just a cost on the balance sheet until something pulls intelligence out of the data it produces. That gap is exactly what our project closes.

03 — Strategic assessment

SWOT: the moat is the module, the risk is the transition

StrengthsHardware rivals can't match

Inertial nav bypasses GPS jamming exactly where ride density and revenue peak; 45% share is a real data moat.

WeaknessesThe transition is the risk

The legacy fleet still goes blind in dense cities, and rollout means running two hardware generations at once.

OpportunitiesPlenty of room to grow

A long pipeline of 100k+ cities, plus a fragmented LatAm market — each launch amortizes the module R&D.

ThreatsSpeed beats proof

Super-app rivals win on distribution; one high-profile accident can trigger city bans before the tech proves itself.

SMART goal — Safety First

Scale the 7th-gen module fleet-wide: cut module cost 20%, double the battery, equip 18,000+ scooters, and keep tracking continuous in GPS-blind zones — all within the 2025 season.

→ The same module also produces navigation data — which feeds a second objective entirely: expansion. Hold that thought.

05 — Candidate projects

Three ways to turn module data into advantage

Digital Twin

A live profile of every scooter — hardware gen, battery, maintenance. Tames the two-generation transition.

≈15–20% lower maintenance cost

Safety Control

Turns CV detections — tandem rides, crosswalk violations — into dashboards and regulator-ready evidence.

≈30% fewer repeat violations

we'll come back to this one

Nav Data Platform

Turns GPS and ride data into a city-scoring engine for expansion decisions.

≈20% better launch ROI

07 — The decision

Nav Data Platform wins — cheapest, fastest, lowest risk

4.35/5 Highest weighted score

Why it won

$200–400K · 4–6 months · 85–90% success

  • No real-time dependencies — the data already exists in the modules, we just have to structure it.
  • Foundational: it builds the data layer the other two projects can later sit on top of.

The honest trade-off

Our goal is safety — but the 7th-gen rollout already is the safety project, funded and running. So among the three software projects we chose on foundational value and feasibility.

Safety Control won on safety-fit (5/5 vs 3/5) — but 2× the cost and more risk on a fleet mid-transition.

Not dropped — sequenced as Phase 2.

08 — The Nav Data Platform

What we're building: score cities, guide expansion

S
Build a platform that scores and ranks new city launches from GPS quality, ride demand, and revenue data.
M
Score 15+ candidate cities (5+ in LatAm). Cut evaluation from weeks to days. Target +20% launch ROI.
A
Data already flows from 7th-gen modules. Needs 2–3 engineers + 1 analyst on existing infra — no new hardware.
R
Directly serves geographic expansion, and validates the 7th-gen R&D by turning hardware data into strategy.
T
MVP in 3 months; full platform with LatAm coverage in 4–6. Within the 2025 season.

Three gut-feel decisions become data-driven:

  • Which city to launch — scored on GPS, demand and competition, not guessed.
  • Where 7th-gen modules go first — to the worst GPS zones, not by schedule.
  • How the expansion budget is split — follows projected return per city, not a top-down number.

09 — Cost, risk & value

The numbers behind the recommendation

~$280K total cost — people + infra + 15% contingency
~$160K/yr saved waste — the conservative computed floor
$400–600K per year incl. revenue upside (est.)

How the floor is computed

4 launches/yr × $200K × 20% uplift = ~$160K/yr saved. Better selection lifts it toward $400–600K. Pays back under two years on the floor, under one if the upside lands.

10 — Roadmap

This is Phase 1 of three

  1. 1

    Phase 1 — now

    Nav Data Platform

    The data foundation. Cheap and fast — it builds the layer everything else sits on.

  2. 2

    Phase 2 — after rollout stabilizes

    Safety Control

    The compliance layer — it reuses Phase 1's pipeline, cloud and data team, so it starts from a platform already paid for.

  3. 3

    Phase 3 — full fleet on 7th-gen

    Digital Twin

    The operations layer — a live profile of every scooter, when the budget allows.

All three feed the same vision — the 15-minute city. And the order matters: each phase makes the next one cheaper and less risky.
